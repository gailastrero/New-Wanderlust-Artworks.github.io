Vector Line Art

Vector art is created using vector illustration software programs, such as Adobe Illustrator or Corel Draw. These programs use mathematic equations and geometric primitives (points, lines, and shapes) to create art that is clean, camera ready, and can be scaled infinitely, without any loss of quality or fidelity.

Splattering Artwork

A method to create random, small dots of paint. Load a small amount of paint onto a brush or, even better, an old toothbrush, then flick it towards the paper. Running a palette knife through the brush to flick the paint can work well.

Burning Effect

Burn Effect Artwork

Dodging and burning are terms used in photography for a technique used during the printing process to manipulate the exposure of a selected area(s) on a photographic print, deviating from the rest of the image's exposure. In a darkroom print from a film negative, dodging decreases the exposure for areas of the print that the photographer wishes to be lighter, while burning increases the exposure to areas of the print that should be darker.
